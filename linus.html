Linus Torvalds

The creator of the Linux Kernel Operating System

Linus Torvalds is the software engineer who created the Linux Kernel Operating System.

Here is a timeline of Linus Torvalds life:

  • 1969 - Born in Helsinki, Finland.
  • 1988 - Begins studying at the University of Helsinki.
  • 1991 - Purchased the Intel 80386-based IBM PC and obtained a MINIX operating system. With this, he began to work and research on the Linux OS.
  • 1996 - Obtains his master's degree in computer science.
  • 1998 - Received the EFF Pioneer Award.
  • 1999 - He was offered stock options for his creation by the companies, Red Hat and VA Linux. That very year, the stock prices increased to about $20 million after the companies went public.
  • 2004 - He, along with his family, relocated to Beaverton, Oregon, where Open Source Development Labs was headquartered. The following year, he developed the software, 'Git'.
  • 2006 - About two percent of the Linux Kernel was written by him, which is a significant contribution.
  • 2012 - He stated that his contribution to the Linux kernel mostly involved merging codes that were already written by others and did not involve much programming.
